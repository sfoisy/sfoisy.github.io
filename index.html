About

Scientist.
Storyteller.
Sky watcher.

I'm a PhD candidate at Columbia University / NASA GISS studying atmospheric aerosols — the tiny particles suspended in the air that scatter sunlight, seed clouds, and quietly steer Earth's climate.

My work uses satellite data from NASA's PACE mission and ground-based fieldwork to understand how aerosols behave, where they come from, and how we should represent them in the models we use to project our future climate.

I believe science only matters if people understand it — so alongside my research, I make videos, write, and talk to anyone who will listen about what's happening in our atmosphere.
