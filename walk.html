270 Miles on Foot

Last spring, I walked from New York City to Washington, D.C. On foot, the whole way: 270.4 miles, to carry one simple message to the people who set its budget. Weather and climate science is worth funding.

Along the route I stopped to meet 20 climate and weather scientists at 12 institutions, sitting down with each of them to talk about the work they do and why it matters to anyone who checks a forecast, breathes the air, or lives near a coast. The walk ended on the steps of the U.S. Capitol, where I held a press conference and spoke with seven members of Congress.

This is the story of that walk, and the people I met along the way.
